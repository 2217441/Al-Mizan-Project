## Overview

This glossary defines key Islamic and technical terms used throughout the Al-Mizan project. All documentation should use these definitions consistently.

## Islamic Terminology

### Primary Sources (Tier 1 - Immutable)

| Term | Arabic | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|--------|-----------------|------------|
| **Quran** | القرآن | Al-Qur'ān | The holy book of Islam, revealed to Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) through Angel Jibril. Considered the literal word of Allah and immutable. |
| **Hadith** | حديث | Ḥadīth | A report of the sayings, actions, or tacit approvals of Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ). Divided into Sahih (authentic), Hasan (good), Da'if (weak), and Mawdu' (fabricated). |
| **Hadith Qudsi** | حديث قدسي | Ḥadīth Qudsī | A hadith where the Prophet conveys words directly from Allah, distinct from Quran in transmission method. |
| **Hadith Nabawi** | حديث نبوي | Ḥadīth Nabawī | A hadith containing the Prophet's own words (not direct divine speech). |
| **Sunnah** | سنة | Sunnah | The normative practice and traditions of Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ), derived from hadith. |

---

### Hadith Sciences

| Term | Arabic | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|--------|-----------------|------------|
| **Isnad** | إسناد | Isnād | The chain of narrators transmitting a hadith from the Prophet to the compiler. Critical for authenticity assessment. |
| **Matn** | متن | Matn | The actual text/content of a hadith, as opposed to its chain of transmission. |
| **Rawi** | راوي | Rāwī | A narrator in the chain of hadith transmission. Plural: *Ruwat* (رواة). |
| **Sahih** | صحيح | Ṣaḥīḥ | Authentic; the highest grade of hadith reliability. |
| **Hasan** | حسن | Ḥasan | Good; acceptable hadith with slightly weaker chain than Sahih. |
| **Da'if** | ضعيف | Ḍa'īf | Weak; hadith with defects in chain or content. |
| **Mawdu'** | موضوع | Mawḍū' | Fabricated; hadith proven to be invented. Cannot be used for rulings. |
| **Tabaqat** | طبقات | Ṭabaqāt | Generational classes of hadith narrators (e.g., Companions, Successors). |

### Jurisprudence (Fiqh)

| Term | Arabic | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|--------|-----------------|------------|
| **Fiqh** | فقه | Fiqh | Islamic jurisprudence; the human understanding and application of Shariah. |
| **Hukm** | حكم | Ḥukm | Legal ruling/verdict. Plural: *Ahkam* (أحكام). |
| **Wajib** | واجب | Wājib | Obligatory; an action that must be performed (sin if omitted). |
| **Mandub** | مندوب | Mandūb | Recommended; an action that is rewarded if done but not sinful if omitted. Also: *Mustahabb* (مستحب). |
| **Mubah** | مباح | Mubāḥ | Permissible; an action that is neither rewarded nor punished. |
| **Makruh** | مكروه | Makrūh | Disliked; an action that is better to avoid but not sinful. |
| **Haram** | حرام | Ḥarām | Forbidden; an action that is sinful and must be avoided. |
| **Halal** | حلال | Ḥalāl | Permissible; opposite of Haram. |

### Schools of Thought

| Term | Arabic | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|--------|-----------------|------------|
| **Madhab** | مذهب | Madhhab | School of Islamic jurisprudence. Plural: *Madhahib* (مذاهب). |
| **Hanafi** | حنفي | Ḥanafī | School founded by Imam Abu Hanifa (d. 767 CE). Emphasizes reason (*ra'y*) and analogy (*qiyas*). |
| **Maliki** | مالكي | Mālikī | School founded by Imam Malik (d. 795 CE). Emphasizes practice of Medina. |
| **Shafi'i** | شافعي | Shāfi'ī | School founded by Imam al-Shafi'i (d. 820 CE). Systematized *usul al-fiqh*. |
| **Hanbali** | حنبلي | Ḥanbalī | School founded by Imam Ahmad ibn Hanbal (d. 855 CE). Emphasizes textual evidence. |

### Sources of Law

| Term | Arabic | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|--------|-----------------|------------|
| **Ijma'** | إجماع | Ijmā' | Scholarly consensus; agreement of qualified scholars on a ruling. |
| **Qiyas** | قياس | Qiyās | Analogical reasoning; deriving rulings by comparing to established cases. |
| **Ijtihad** | اجتهاد | Ijtihād | Independent legal reasoning by a qualified scholar (*mujtahid*). |
| **Taqlid** | تقليد | Taqlīd | Following the opinion of a scholar or school without independent analysis. |
| **Naskh** | نسخ | Naskh | Abrogation; when a later verse supersedes an earlier ruling. |

## Technical Terminology

### Database & Graph

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **SurrealDB** | The graph-document database used by Al-Mizan. Supports both relational and graph queries. |
| **SurQL** | SurrealDB Query Language; used for querying the knowledge graph. |
| **Node** | A vertex in the knowledge graph representing an entity (verse, hadith, scholar, etc.). |
| **Edge** | A relationship between two nodes (e.g., `derived_from`, `narrated_by`). |
| **RELATION** | A typed edge in SurrealDB with its own properties. |
| **Record ID** | Unique identifier in format `table:id` (e.g., `quran_verse:1_1`). |

### Architecture

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Tier 1 Data** | Immutable primary sources: Quran and authenticated Hadith. Read-only. |
| **Tier 2 Data** | Scholarly interpretations: Fiqh rulings. Mutable with attribution. |
| **Tier 3 Data** | Contextual information: Historical, linguistic, biographical data. |
| **Tawhidic Foundation** | The ontological hierarchy: Allah → Divine Names → Prophets → Revelation → Knowledge. |
| **Isnad Graph** | The chain-of-transmission subgraph for hadith authenticity. |

### Identity & Credentials

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **DID** | Decentralized Identifier; a self-sovereign identity standard. |
| **VC** | Verifiable Credential; a cryptographically signed attestation. |
| **did:key** | A DID method where the identifier is derived from a public key. |

### Governance

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Stake & Slash** | Governance mechanism where scholars stake reputation; slashing occurs for invalid rulings. |
| **Byzantine Scholar** | A scholar who has been permanently banned after 3 slashing incidents. |
| **Probationary** | Status of a ruling or scholar pending verification. |
| **Canonical** | Status of an accepted, verified ruling. |

## Usage Guidelines

### Capitalization

- **Capitalize**: Quran, Hadith (when referring to the collections), Allah, Prophet Muhammad, Divine Names
- **Lowercase**: hadith (individual report), fiqh (discipline), madhab (generic)

### First Reference

On first use in a document, provide the Arabic and transliteration:
> "Fiqh (فقه, *fiqh*) is the Islamic science of jurisprudence..."

### Code References

Use `backticks` for:
- Table names: `quran_verse`, `hadith`
- Field names: `matn_ar`, `hukm`
- Record IDs: `scholar:ibn_taymiyyah`

### Abbreviations

| Abbreviation | Meaning | Usage |
|--------------|---------|-------|
| (ﷺ) | *ṣallā Allāhu 'alayhi wa sallam* | After Prophet Muhammad's name |
| (AS) | *'alayhi al-salām* | After other prophets' names |
| (RA) | *raḍiya Allāhu 'anhu/hā* | After companions' names |
| AH | *After Hijra* | Islamic calendar dates |
| CE | *Common Era* | Gregorian calendar dates |

## Quick Reference

### The Five Rulings (Al-Ahkam al-Khamsa)

```
Wajib → Mandub → Mubah → Makruh → Haram
(Obligatory)  (Recommended)  (Permissible)  (Disliked)  (Forbidden)
```

### Data Tier Hierarchy

```
Tier 1: Quran + Sahih Hadith (IMMUTABLE)
    ↓
Tier 2: Fiqh Rulings (MUTABLE with attribution)
    ↓
Tier 3: Context (Historical, Linguistic)
```

### Hadith Grades

```
Sahih > Hasan > Da'if > Mawdu'
(Authentic)  (Good)    (Weak)    (Fabricated)
```
